Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a rich and comforting Italian casserole made by alternating layers of wide, flat pasta sheets, a savory meat or vegetable sauce (usually a rich Bolognese), and a creamy cheese filling (often Ricotta or Bechamel sauce), topped with a generous amount of Mozzarella and Parmesan cheese. It's a perfect dish for a family dinner or a special occasion.

Ingredients

For the Sauce (Bolognese)

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
  • 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (15oz) can tomato sauce
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper

For the Cheese Filling

  • 1 (15 oz) container Ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper

For Layering

  • 9 lasagna noodles (oven-ready or regular, follow package directions for cooking)
  • 3 cups (about 12 oz) shredded Mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Make the Meat Sauce (Bolognese)
    1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot or skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
    2. Add the ground meat and cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until it's browned. Drain any excess fat.
    3. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
    4. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and tomato sauce. Add the oregano, basil, salt, and pepper.
    5. Bring the sauce to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it cook for at least 30 minutes (up to 1 hour for richer flavor), stirring occasionally.

  2. Prepare the Noodles (If Needed)
    1. you are using regular lasagna noodles (not oven-ready), cook them according to the package directions until al dente. Drain them and lay them flat on a sheet of parchment paper or a clean kitchen towel to prevent sticking.

  3. Mix the Ricotta Filling
    1. In a medium bowl, combine the Ricotta cheese, egg, fresh parsley, salt, and pepper. Mix thoroughly until all ingredients are well combined.

  4. Preheat and Prepare
    1. Preheat your oven to 375∘F (≈190∘C).
    2. Lightly grease a 9×13-inch (or similar size) baking dish.

  5. Assemble the Lasagna
    1. First Layer (Sauce): Spread about 1 cup of the meat sauce evenly across the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
    2. Second Layer (Noodles): Lay 3 lasagna noodles lengthwise over the sauce.
    3. Third Layer (Ricotta): Spread half of the Ricotta mixture evenly over the noodles.
    4. Fourth Layer (Meat & Cheese): Spread about 1/3 of the remaining meat sauce over the Ricotta, then sprinkle with about 1 cup of Mozzarella cheese
    5. Repeat: Add 3 more noodles, the rest of the Ricotta mixture, another 1/3 of the remaining meat sauce, and another 1 cup of Mozzarella.
    6. Final Layer: Top with the last 3 noodles, the remaining meat sauce, and the last cup of Mozzarella and the Parmesan cheese.

  6. Bake and Serve
    1. Cover the baking dish tightly with aluminum foil.
    2. Bake for 25 minutes.
    3. Remove the foil and continue baking for another 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly, golden brown, and the lasagna is heated through.
    4. Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving. This helps it set and makes it much easier to serve neatly.
